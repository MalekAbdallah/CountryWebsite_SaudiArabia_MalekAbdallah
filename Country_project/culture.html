A Blend of Tradition and Modern Life

Saudi culture is deeply rooted in Arab heritage, Islamic values, hospitality, and a strong connection to family and community. While modern cities grow rapidly, traditions continue to shape the identity of the Kingdom.

Traditional Clothing

Men typically wear the thobe, while women wear the abaya. In many regions, traditional patterns and colors vary, representing local heritage.

Food & Cuisine

Saudi cuisine features rich flavors, spices, and hospitality. Popular dishes include:

  • Kabsa: The national dish made of rice, meat, and spices.
  • Jareesh: A traditional wheat-based dish.
  • Dates & Arabic Coffee: A symbol of hospitality across the Kingdom.

Music & Dance

Traditional Saudi music includes drums, poetry, and regional rhythms. The Ardah, a sword dance performed with chanting, is one of the most iconic cultural traditions.

Modern Culture

Saudi Arabia now hosts concerts, film festivals, art exhibitions, and major sporting events. Cities such as Riyadh and Jeddah have become centers of entertainment and creativity.
