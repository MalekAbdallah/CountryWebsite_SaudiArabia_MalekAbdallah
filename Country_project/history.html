From Ancient Civilizations to a Modern Kingdom

The Arabian Peninsula has been home to tribes, trade routes, and ancient civilizations for thousands of years. Long before the rise of Islam, Arabia was a crossroads of culture, linking Africa, Asia, and Europe.

Early Civilizations

Regions like AlUla, Najran, and the eastern coast hosted thriving ancient communities. The famous Nabateans built stone cities such as Hegra, which is now a UNESCO World Heritage site.

The Birth of Islam (7th Century)

In 610 CE, Prophet Muhammad received the first revelation in Mecca, leading to the birth of Islam. Within decades, the Arabian tribes united under the new religion, shaping the region for centuries.

The First and Second Saudi States

The Al Saud family established the first Saudi state in 1744, followed by a second in the 19th century. These early states laid the foundation for the modern nation.

Unification in 1932

King Abdulaziz Al Saud unified the regions of the peninsula into what is now the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia on September 23, 1932.

Modern Era & Vision 2030

Today, Saudi Arabia is rapidly expanding with economic, cultural, and technological advancements that are transforming the kingdom into a global hub.
